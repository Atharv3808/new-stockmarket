What is Options Trading?

Options Trading, in general, involves purchasing or selling an underlying asset at a predetermined or pre-negotiated price by a specified future date.

10 Crucial Pieces of Knowledge Before Options Trading

  1. Options Trading Fundamentals: Options trading offers a diverse range of maturities, including one-, two-, and three-month expirations. In the case of indices, long-term options are also available. Additionally, various platforms provide options such as weekly options.
  2. Call and Put Options: Call and put options allow for both buying and selling. In most situations, the option buyer possesses the potential right to exercise these options without any obligation or stress. The option buyer pays a premium to the option seller in exchange for this privilege, which is not contingent upon future events.
  3. Premium and Loss Potential: The premium paid for a call or put option serves as the maximum loss potential. This premium is typically paid upfront and does not incur any future obligations. However, the premium paid is the basis for the potential benefits of the option.
  4. Initial Investment and Profitability: Initial investment in options is usually limited to the premium paid. The premium paid for a call or put option effectively caps the potential loss, which can significantly impact the overall growth rate and MTM margins, which are typically owed in the case of futures trading. Option buyers do not have to worry about these obligations.
  5. Trade-Offs in Option Trading: Each option acquisition involves a trade-off. Options with shorter remaining maturities are offered at a reduced premium. This premium reduction is only possible before the expiration date. However, a shorter maturity also reduces the potential profit from the option and may render it null.
  6. Bigul: Your Optimal Trading Partner: Bigul is the premier trading platform in India, offering a comprehensive range of options trading services. With Bigul, you can invest and earn substantial profits while enjoying the benefits of a user-friendly platform.
  7. Volatility: The Key to Option Pricing: Volatility is a pivotal factor influencing the value of options. The correlation between call and put options is often favourable, benefiting traders. Notably, both calls and puts appreciate during market volatility, presenting opportunities for trading and investing.
  8. Advantages of Options Trading in India: Securities Transaction Tax (STT) calculation in options differs from futures trading. While futures are based on notional value, options are calculated on premium value.
  9. Essential Concepts for Options Trading: The option price for a specific strike encompasses two components: stock value and time value. Options traders must grasp these concepts. When initiating options trading, it is crucial to select the most suitable options from the market. Bigul is the ideal platform for this purpose.
  10. Understanding the Immediate and Intrinsic Value of Options: In the dynamic landscape of options trading, grasping the immediate and intrinsic value of options is paramount. It is essential to recognise that options are considered a wasting asset, and their time value diminishes as the maturity date approaches. This decline can reach a point where it virtually vanishes. Consequently, for option buyers, purchasing options before the contract’s commencement offers a greater advantage, providing ample time value and volatility prospects for market analysis and strategic decision-making.

Options: A Versatile and Dynamic Asset

Options are highly versatile and dynamic assets, making them an intriguing investment tool. Their adaptability allows for various trading strategies. For instance, call options can be utilised to speculate on the stock price of a company like Reliance Industries (RIL), anticipating an increase. Similarly, put options can be employed to speculate on a decline in the stock price of a company such as State Bank of India (SBI).
