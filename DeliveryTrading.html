What is Delivery Trading?

Delivery trading is a stock market strategy where investors buy shares and take delivery of them into their Demat account, holding them beyond the trading day. The investor retains ownership until deciding to sell‚Äîwhether to realize a profit or manage a loss.

Unlike intraday trading, where positions are squared off within the same day, delivery trading involves no time-bound transactions. It‚Äôs ideal for those looking to invest in fundamentally strong companies and grow their capital over time.

Disadvantages of Delivery Trading

Despite its merits, delivery trading also carries some downsides:

  • ‚ùå Market Risk Exposure
    Since positions are held over time, they are subject to broader market risks and fluctuations due to economic, political, or company-specific events.

  • ‚ùå Dependency on Market Trends

How to Start Delivery Trading: Step-by-Step

Starting delivery trading is simple. Follow these steps:

  1. Open a Demat Account
    This is where your shares will be stored electronically. You can open a Demat account with a SEBI-registered depository participant (DP) such as Zerodha, Upstox, or traditional banks.
  2. Open a Trading Account
    A trading account is needed to place buy and sell orders. Most brokers provide integrated Demat + Trading account services.
  3. Fund Your Account
    Transfer funds from your bank account to your trading account through UPI, NEFT, or net banking.
  4. Research and Analyze Stocks
    Use company reports, market news, and analysis tools to shortlist stocks based on your investment objectives.
  5. Place an Order
    Choose between:
    Market Order ‚Äì executes immediately at current price.
    Limit Order ‚Äì executes only at your specified price or better.
  6. Receive Delivery
    Stocks bought for delivery will be transferred to your Demat account on a T+2 settlement basis (Trade date + 2 working days).
  7. Track and Hold
    Monitor company performance periodically, stay informed with market news, and hold your positions as per your strategy.
